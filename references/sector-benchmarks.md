# US Small-Cap Sector Benchmarks

Use these ranges as starting points, not laws. Compare each company against its own sub-sector, maturity, and capital intensity. A company below the weak range needs a hard explanation. A company above the strong range needs verification from filings, not a slide deck.

All margin figures are percentages of revenue. Debt/EBITDA and interest coverage use trailing twelve-month figures unless annual data is more reliable.

---

## 1. Semiconductors

Covers fabless chip designers, integrated device manufacturers, semiconductor equipment, advanced packaging, photonics, power semis, and specialty component suppliers.

| Metric | Weak | Average | Strong |
|--------|------|---------|--------|
| Gross Margin | <35% | 35-55% | >55% |
| EBITDA Margin | <5% | 10-25% | >25% |
| Net Margin | <0% | 5-18% | >18% |
| FCF Margin | <0% | 5-15% | >15% |
| ROE | <5% | 8-18% | >18% |
| Debt/EBITDA | >3.0x | 1.0-3.0x | <1.0x or net cash |
| Interest Coverage | <2.5x | 3.0-8.0x | >8.0x |
| Revenue Growth | <-5% | 0-15% | >15% |
| P/E | >45x or negative | 18-35x | <18x with stable growth |
| EV/Revenue | >8.0x with weak margins | 2.0-6.0x | <2.0x with positive FCF |

Sub-sector notes:

- Fabless: strong businesses often show gross margin above 55% and lower capex, but inventory, channel stuffing, and customer concentration can still wreck earnings.
- IDMs: lower gross margin may be acceptable if capacity utilization is high and capex is disciplined. Check depreciation and maintenance capex.
- Equipment: backlog quality matters. A high margin equipment company with a shrinking order book is usually late-cycle, not cheap.
- Power semis and photonics: design wins can take years to scale. Verify production revenue, not just sampling or qualification.

Red flags:

- Inventory grows faster than revenue for two consecutive quarters.
- One customer exceeds 25% of revenue without a multi-year supply agreement.
- Adjusted EBITDA excludes recurring stock-based compensation and restructuring every year.
- China export controls create a revenue hole that management describes with percentages but not dollars.

## 2. AI and Cloud Infrastructure

Covers data centers, GPU hosting, colocation, AI cloud providers, compute-as-a-service, power-campus developers, and infrastructure companies selling AI capacity.

| Metric | Weak | Average | Strong |
|--------|------|---------|--------|
| Gross Margin | <25% | 30-50% | >50% |
| EBITDA Margin | <10% | 15-35% | >35% |
| Net Margin | <0% | 0-12% | >12% |
| FCF Margin | <-20% | -20% to 5% | >5% |
| ROE | <0% | 5-12% | >12% |
| Debt/EBITDA | >5.0x | 2.5-5.0x | <2.5x |
| Interest Coverage | <1.5x | 2.0-5.0x | >5.0x |
| Revenue Growth | <10% | 20-60% | >60% with funded capex |
| P/E | Negative or >60x | 25-50x | <25x with FCF path |
| EV/Revenue | >12.0x | 4.0-10.0x | <4.0x with contracted growth |

Sub-sector notes:

- Data center owners: EBITDA margin can look strong while FCF is awful because capex absorbs the economics. EV/EBITDA alone is dangerous.
- GPU hosting: gross margin depends on utilization, financing cost, power cost, depreciation period, and customer contract length. A two-year customer contract against five-year debt is a mismatch.
- Power-campus developers: booked megawatts are not revenue. Check interconnection status, transformer delivery, permitting, and committed financing.

Red flags:

- Announced megawatts are not separated into owned, leased, powered, contracted, and revenue-generating.
- GPU count is disclosed without utilization or customer pricing.
- Capex commitments exceed cash and committed debt facilities.
- Customer contracts are called “long-term” without term, minimum revenue, termination rights, and credit quality.

## 3. Crypto Miners and HPC Pivot Stories

Covers BTC miners, mining infrastructure owners, power operators, and miners pivoting into high-performance computing or AI hosting.

| Metric | Weak | Average | Strong |
|--------|------|---------|--------|
| Gross Margin | <20% | 25-50% | >50% |
| EBITDA Margin | <5% | 15-40% | >40% |
| Net Margin | <0% | 0-15% | >15% across cycle |
| FCF Margin | <-30% | -30% to 5% | >5% through cycle |
| ROE | <0% | 5-15% | >15% across cycle |
| Debt/EBITDA | >4.0x | 1.5-4.0x | <1.5x or net cash |
| Interest Coverage | <1.5x | 2.0-5.0x | >5.0x |
| Revenue Growth | <0% | 10-50% | >50% with hashprice discipline |
| P/E | Negative or >40x | 12-30x | <12x with positive FCF |
| EV/Revenue | >8.0x | 2.0-6.0x | <2.0x with low power cost |

Operational benchmarks:

- Fleet efficiency: weak above 30 J/TH, average 20-30 J/TH, strong below 20 J/TH.
- Power cost: weak above $0.055/kWh, average $0.035-$0.055/kWh, strong below $0.035/kWh.
- Direct cost per BTC: compare against spot BTC and network difficulty. A miner that looks profitable at $90,000 BTC may be structurally weak at $55,000 BTC.

HPC pivot checks:

- Real HPC revenue requires power, cooling, fiber, security, uptime guarantees, customer contracts, and capex. A mining shed is not automatically an AI data center.
- Check whether AI/HPC contracts are binding, minimum-revenue, creditworthy, and financed.
- Separate hosting revenue from self-mining revenue.

Red flags:

- Adjusted EBITDA excludes BTC impairment, machine impairment, stock compensation, and financing costs, leaving a fantasy number.
- Miner sells BTC every month to fund operations but calls the balance sheet a treasury strategy.
- HPC pivot uses letters of intent instead of signed contracts.
- ASIC-backed debt or vendor financing matures before machines earn back cost.

## 4. Growth Tech

Covers SaaS, adtech, fintech, marketplaces, vertical software, data platforms, and other capital-light technology companies.

| Metric | Weak | Average | Strong |
|--------|------|---------|--------|
| Gross Margin | <45% | 55-75% | >75% |
| EBITDA Margin | <-20% | -10% to 20% | >20% |
| Net Margin | <-25% | -10% to 15% | >15% |
| FCF Margin | <-20% | -5% to 15% | >15% |
| ROE | <0% | 5-20% | >20% |
| Debt/EBITDA | >3.0x or EBITDA negative | 1.0-3.0x | <1.0x or net cash |
| Interest Coverage | <2.0x | 3.0-8.0x | >8.0x |
| Revenue Growth | <10% | 15-35% | >35% |
| P/E | Negative or >60x | 25-45x | <25x with durable growth |
| EV/Revenue | >10.0x | 3.0-8.0x | <3.0x with positive FCF |

Quality metrics:

- Net revenue retention: weak below 100%, average 105-120%, strong above 120%.
- Gross retention: weak below 85%, average 85-92%, strong above 92%.
- Rule of 40: weak below 20, average 20-40, strong above 40.
- CAC payback: weak above 24 months, average 12-24 months, strong below 12 months.
- SBC/revenue: weak above 20%, average 8-20%, strong below 8%.

Red flags:

- Revenue growth is bought with sales and marketing spend that does not improve retention.
- Adjusted profit excludes stock-based compensation that exceeds FCF.
- Customer concentration is hidden behind platform or partner revenue.
- Net revenue retention is omitted after it deteriorates.

## 5. Small-Cap Biotech

Covers preclinical, clinical-stage, and early commercial biotech, diagnostics, platform drug developers, and specialty pharma with high binary risk.

Financial statement benchmarks are different because many companies have little or no revenue. For pre-revenue biotech, valuation depends on cash, burn, asset quality, trial probability, and financing risk.

| Metric | Weak | Average | Strong |
|--------|------|---------|--------|
| Gross Margin | <50% for commercial products | 60-80% | >80% |
| EBITDA Margin | Negative with <4 qtrs runway | Negative with 4-8 qtrs runway | Positive or funded through key data |
| Net Margin | Deeply negative with no catalyst | Negative but funded | Positive or near breakeven |
| FCF Margin | Negative with unfunded trials | Negative but planned | Positive for commercial stage |
| ROE | Not meaningful if loss-making | Not meaningful | >15% for commercial stage |
| Debt/EBITDA | Any debt with no revenue is weak | Convertible or royalty debt manageable if funded | Net cash, no debt |
| Interest Coverage | <1.0x | 1.0-3.0x if commercial | >3.0x if commercial |
| Revenue Growth | No product or shrinking grants | Early launch growth 20-60% | >60% with gross margin support |
| P/E | Usually not meaningful | 20-40x for profitable specialty pharma | <20x with durable product |
| EV/Revenue | >12.0x for weak launch | 4.0-10.0x | <4.0x with growth and cash |

Biotech-specific benchmarks:

- Cash runway: weak below 4 quarters, average 4-8 quarters, strong funded through the next major readout or PDUFA plus 2 quarters.
- Single-asset dependence: weak if one failed asset leaves no viable pipeline, average if two independent shots, strong if multiple funded programs with different mechanisms.
- Trial stage: preclinical is highest risk, Phase 1 safety only, Phase 2 signal-seeking, Phase 3 pivotal, PDUFA binary.
- Dilution risk: weak if market cap is below expected 12-month burn, average if 12-24 months of burn covered, strong if partnerships or product revenue fund trials.

Red flags:

- Company raises equity shortly after positive data without explaining why the data did not attract partnership capital.
- Endpoint changes, subgroup claims, or post hoc analysis drive the bull case.
- Cash runs out before the next data readout.
- Insider selling or option grants cluster around trial announcements.
- FDA correspondence is summarized in promotional terms without filing the actual substance.
